TILAK SANSKAR

In Hinduism Traditional, tilak sanskar is also called the Groom acceptance ceremony. It is vital a process of acceptance of the groom as a competent person to marry the girl. This is the most important ritual which has it's a significant position in Indian marriage. The tilak sanskar is also known as pottu or chenkai in south India and teep in the eastern parts of India. It follows the process quite differently in different parts of the country. In general, Tilak is held to the groom's residence. On the whole process, you can say that it is the shart of the ice-breaking ceremony between the bride and the groom's families.
HOW THE RITUALS OF TILAK CEREMONY HOLD:
The special type of thali is used in the Tilak sanskar which consists of different components depending on the culture or region of the respective families. The main products used in the Tilak ceremony are- tilak, saffron, kumkum, oil, sandalwood and a Diya.
● The tilak sanskar begins with a pooja or havan.
● The father of the bride puts tilak on the forehead of the groom.
● After the pooja, the bride's father offers some gifts to the groom and his family.
